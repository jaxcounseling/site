About Us

Jax counseling is run by Dr. Denise Meisburg who holds a Doctorate in Pastoral Counseling and a Masters of Religious Arts. She is ordained as a minister from New Life International and for the last 16 years has acted as a Director of Prayer and Counseling for the International Humanitarian Ministry. She has worked as a *RN for over 32 years, specializing in hospice-end of life care, psychiatric, rehabilitative and detox nursing. She is also certified to work with high risk teens. She has over 35+ years of marriage and six wonderful kids.

Dr. Denise Meisburg

We integrate the strength of a person’s faith together with insight from many modalities of counseling, utilizing relevant biblical principles to help clients toward health and wholeness. We provide help with the formulation of goals that leads the individual to break self-destructive habits and gain control of negative emotions. We enable the experience of real relief from anxiety, fears, despair, bitterness, anger, confusion, loneliness, grief and depression. The application of truth from God’s Word to the individual life, addresses the biological, psychological, sociological, and spiritual issues concurrently, utilizing instruction, discussion and prayer.

We strive to help emotionally wounded people find freedom and healing through a personal encounter with the Living God. We transform maladaptive patterns of thinking into adaptive, truth based thinking and reinforces constructive behaviors. We identify behaviors that tend to maintain emotional distress and reduce or eliminate such behaviors. We teach healthier patterns of relating to others and identify internal conflicts and psychological defenses, providing insight into their causes. We help clients uncover unconscious motivations and repressed memories and become aware of how these affect feelings and behavior.

Note

* This in no way represents that I am practicing nursing or medicine, or am a state licensed psychologist or state licensed clinical counselor. My Focus is Pastoral Christian Counseling from a Biblical perspective.
